# 30-Day Exam Study Plan Template (With Daily Targets)

A 30-day study plan gives you enough time to cover material thoroughly while maintaining momentum. This template provides daily targets and a structured approach to exam preparation.

## Why 30 Days Works

**30 days is ideal because:**
- Enough time for multiple passes through material
- Allows for spaced repetition
- Prevents last-minute panic
- Builds confidence gradually
- Accommodates unexpected events

**Too short (7-14 days):** Rushed, stressful, incomplete coverage
**Too long (60+ days):** Loses momentum, hard to maintain focus

## The 30-Day Structure

### Phase 1: Foundation (Days 30-21)
**Goal:** Initial learning and understanding
**Focus:** Building knowledge base

### Phase 2: Practice (Days 20-11)
**Goal:** Application and problem-solving
**Focus:** Deep practice and testing

### Phase 3: Review (Days 10-1)
**Goal:** Consolidation and confidence
**Focus:** Review, past papers, final prep

## Daily Study Targets

### Study Hours Per Day

**Days 30-21:** 3-4 hours/day
- Focus on learning new material
- Build foundation
- Regular but not overwhelming

**Days 20-11:** 4-5 hours/day
- Intensive practice
- Problem-solving
- Past papers

**Days 10-1:** 2-3 hours/day
- Review and consolidation
- Light practice
- Rest and recovery

### Daily Structure

**Morning (2-3 hours):**
- New material or intensive practice
- Peak focus time
- Difficult topics

**Afternoon (1-2 hours):**
- Review and practice
- Past papers
- Problem-solving

**Evening (30 minutes - 1 hour):**
- Light review
- Formula memorization
- Quick practice

## Customizing the Template

### For Easier Exams:
- Reduce daily hours
- Fewer practice problems
- More review, less new material

### For Difficult Exams:
- Increase daily hours
- More practice problems
- Extra weak area focus
- More past papers

### For Multiple Exams:
- Use [exam countdown](/countdown) for each
- Prioritize by date
- Balance time between exams
- Use [study plan generator](/study-plan) for balance

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Starting Too Late
**Problem:** Only 2 weeks before exam
**Solution:** Start 30 days out, adjust template

### Mistake 2: No Review Time
**Problem:** Only learning new material
**Solution:** Build in daily and weekly reviews

### Mistake 3: Ignoring Weak Areas
**Problem:** Avoiding difficult topics
**Solution:** Schedule extra time for weak areas

### Mistake 4: No Practice Tests
**Problem:** Only studying, never testing
**Solution:** Include past papers regularly

### Mistake 5: Burning Out
**Problem:** Studying 8 hours/day for 30 days
**Solution:** Balance study with rest

## Tips for Success

### 1. Use the Countdown Tool
Track days remaining with [exam countdown](/countdown) for motivation and planning.

### 2. Break Down Large Topics
Instead of "Study Chapter 1," break into:
- Read section 1.1
- Practice problems 1-10
- Review concepts
- Create notes

### 3. Regular Reviews
Don't just learn new material—review old material regularly.

### 4. Practice Tests
Test yourself regularly, not just at the end.

### 5. Stay Flexible
Adjust the template based on your needs and progress.
